Characteristic

Enrekang Kalosi coffee is well known in the domestic and international markets. This coffee is recognize as special coffee with a distinctive taste and aroma. When it roasted in medium level, it tastes sweet and slightly spicy and also has a very distinctive range of aromas which is a combination of spices, chocolate, fruit, flowers and caramel. Arabica Kalosi Enrekang coffee has been certified organic and must be free of defects..

Environment

The taste of Enrekang Kalosi coffee comes from the interaction between the coffee varieties used and the geographical conditions of the planting, cultivation and processing areas. Traditional methods of local coffee bean production are one of the factors that contribute to the specific quality of Enrekang Kalosi coffee. The geographical area (1,000-2,000 m above sea level, with podzolic soils and cold, dry air), agricultural ecosystem (particularly suitable for Arabica coffee) and climatic conditions of the ancient upland region, combined with the knowledge of the producers, made for high quality coffee.

Production Process

Producers must follow specific techniques to preserve coffee plots (use of natural fertilizers and cover crops, density of plantations, etc.). Ripe cherries are handpicked to ensure the best quality, with 95% red cherries and a maximum of 5% yellow cherries. Green and black cherries are prohibited. To obtain green coffee beans, the cherries are washed (wet processing), sorted, ground, fermented, soaked, sun-dried, sorted and stored. Coffee beans are initially sorted and graded and then hand-sorted to ensure the best quality of the beans. Coffee products from the slopes of the Latimojong Mountains are mostly in the form of green coffee beans (as raw material), only a few are in the form of ground coffee (as the final product). The roasting process does not necessarily take place in the production area.
